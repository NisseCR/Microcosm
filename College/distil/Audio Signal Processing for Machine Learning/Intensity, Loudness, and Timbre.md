**Sound power** is the amount of energy emitted by a source per time unit.

**Intensity** is the amount of sound power per unit area, e.g. W/m^2
It is also measured on a logarithmic scale, using the unit dB (decibels).

**Loudness** is the subjective perception of intensity (like pitch) and measured in **Phons**.
- At lower frequencies, loudness is perceived to be lower.

---

*Timbre*
**Timbre** is the colour of sound (really ambiguous). It is the difference between two sounds that have the same intensity, duration and frequency.

It is defined by many features, hence multidimensional. There are 3 main features that are widely used to define timbre.

*Sound envelope*
Described by attack, decay, sustain and release model. Musical instruments can have different sound envelopes.

![[Pasted image 20260331211449.png]]

*Harmonic content*
A complex sound is a superposition of multiple sinusoids, with such a sinusoid being called a **partial**. The lowest partial (the partial that gives the pitch to a note) is called the **fundamental frequency**, with **harmonic partials** being an integer of that fundamental partial. Harmonic content describes the distribution of energy across these partials, which is part of timbre.

*Frequency modulation*
Also known as **Vibrato**, which is the periodic variation in frequency.

*Amplitude modulation*
Also know as **Tremolo**, which is the periodic variation of amplitude.
