Analog to digital conversion (ADC) is done through two methods:
- **Sampling**
- **Quantisation**

The result of a ADC is an audio-digital signal, also known as a pulse-code modulation.

---

*Sampling*
Sample finite points across the frequency of a waveform. Sampling rate represents the amount of samples per second (measured in Hz).

![[Pasted image 20260331214936.png]]

The **Sampling error** is the area difference between the curve and the sample points.

*Nyquist frequency*
Upper bound frequency in a digital sound that won't create artefacts.

*Aliasing*
Difference between analog sound and the reconstructed sound in frequency, thus creating an artefact.

![[Pasted image 20260331215515.png]]

---

*Quantisation*
Sample finite points across the amplitude of a waveform, measured in resolution or bit depth (number of bits), e.g. 2^16 bits.
